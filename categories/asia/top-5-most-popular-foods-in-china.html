Top 5 Most Popular Foods in China

by Jomari Ondap

Posted on April 14, 2018 at 08:38 PM

Chinese cuisine is an important part of Chinese culture, which includes cuisine originating from the diverse regions of China, as well as from Chinese people in other parts of the world. An average Chinese feast will have two things – a sugar or starch like noodles, rice or buns, and going with mix fries or dishes of veggies, fish and meat. They utilize a ton of crisp vegetables like mushroom, water chestnuts, bamboo and even tofu. In North China, wheat-based backups like noodles and steamed buns command the table, as opposed to South China where rice is a top pick. The short-grain sticky rice, developed all through Southern China, is completely compelling. Because of the Chinese diaspora and historical power of the country, Chinese cuisine has influenced many other cuisines in Asia, with modifications made to cater to local palates. Chinese dishes are famous for color, aroma, taste, meaning, and appearance.

1. Peking Roasted Duck

The first on the list of the Top 5 Most Popular Foods in China is the famous Peking duck. It is a famous dish from Beijing, appreciated around the world, and considered as one of China’s national dishes. Peking duck is famous for its thin and crispy skin. The Sliced Peking duck is often eaten with pancakes, sweet bean sauce, or soy with mashed garlic.

2. Sweet and Sour Pork

The second on the list of the Top 5 Most Popular Foods in China is Sweet and sour pork. Sweet and sour pork has a bright orange-red color and a delicious sweet and sour taste. At the very beginning there was only sweet and sour pork, but to meet demands, there have been some developments on this dish. Now, the pork can be substituted by other ingredients like chicken, beef or pork ribs.

3. Chow Mein

The third on the list of the Top 5 Most Popular Foods in China is Chow mein. Chow mein means stir-fried noodles. Generally speaking, this stir-fried dish consists of noodles, meat (usually chicken, beef, shrimp, or pork), onions and celery. For making chow mein, the noodles need to be cooked in boiling water for a while. After they became cool, then move to the step of stir-frying.

4. Dumplings

The fourth on the list of the Top 5 Most Popular Foods in China is Dumplings. Another one of the most well-known of traditional Chinese foods. this is your classic dumpling, often filled with beef, pork or veggies. consist of minced meat and chopped vegetables wrapped into a thin piece of dough skin. The locals dip their dumplings in black vinegar mixed with a chili sauce, which adds a unique sweet, bitter and spicy flavor.

5. Spring Rolls

And the last but definitely not the least on the list of the Top 5 Most Popular Foods in China is the well-known Spring rolls. Spring rolls are a Cantonese dim sum of cylindrical shape. The filling of spring rolls could be vegetables or meat, and the taste could be either sweet or savory. Spring rolls are a large variety of filled, rolled appetizers or dim sum. After fillings are wrapped in spring roll wrappers, the next step is frying. Then the spring rolls are given their golden yellow color. It is a dish especially popular in Jiangxi, Jiangsu, Shanghai, Fujian, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Hong Kong, etc.
